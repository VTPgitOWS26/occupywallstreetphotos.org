The Project

Between 2011 and 2012, I traveled across the United States documenting Occupy encampments as they emerged in cities large and small.

What began as a photographic journey evolved into a documentary archive preserving one of the defining grassroots movements of the early twenty-first century.

Rather than focusing solely on moments of confrontation, this project documents the people, communities, conversations and everyday life that developed inside the Occupy camps.

Today this website serves as the gateway to the complete archive, organized by individual Occupy encampments and the exact dates on which they were photographed.
